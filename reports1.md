# Operational Assessment and Development Plan

---

## 1. Strengths

Several positive behaviors have been observed, forming a solid foundation for further development:

* **Engagement and Commitment**
  Demonstrates a serious attitude and consistent involvement in assigned tasks.

* **Effective Execution in Structured Contexts**
  Performs well when expectations, processes, and objectives are clearly defined.

* **Operational Reliability**
  Delivers consistent results on well-scoped and straightforward activities.

* **Team Engagement Through Questions**
  Makes visible efforts to interact with team members by asking questions about their work, offering guidance, and providing support. While not always fully structured, this reflects a positive intent to engage and contribute.

* **Genuine Interest in Team Members’ Work**
  Shows authentic curiosity and interest in what others are doing, which is a strong foundation for building trust and effective leadership.

---

## 2. Areas for Improvement

### 2.1 Operational Posture (Proactive vs. Reactive Mindset)

A tendency toward a reactive approach has been observed. Actions are often taken in response to instructions rather than initiated independently.

> **Expected:** Develop a proactive mindset by anticipating needs, identifying gaps, and taking initiative without waiting for direction.

---

### 2.2 Validation Loops (Feedback Loop)

Work is rarely supported by intermediate checkpoints, increasing the risk of misalignment or undetected errors.

> **Expected:** Introduce regular validation points to ensure alignment, quality, and course correction when needed.

---

### 2.3 Operational Leadership (Situational Leadership)

Challenges have been observed in taking full ownership of team coordination, including limited direction-setting and insufficient intervention when needed.

> **Expected:** Strengthen leadership posture by providing clear guidance, supporting team members actively, and stepping in when performance or alignment is at risk.

---

### 2.4 Execution Monitoring

Limited tracking of task progress has been observed, with few follow-ups or structured status checks.

> **Expected:** Actively monitor progress, follow up regularly, and ensure tasks are advancing according to expectations.

---

### 2.5 Communication (Assertive Communication)

Communication remains too passive, with limited proactive updates, clarifications, or escalations.

> **Expected:** Adopt a more assertive communication style by sharing key information, clarifying uncertainties, and raising issues early.

---

### 2.6 Ownership and Accountability

Partial ownership of responsibilities has been observed, with a tendency to focus on task execution rather than overall outcomes.

> **Expected:** Take full responsibility for end results, including anticipating issues and ensuring successful delivery.

---

### 2.7 Critical Thinking

Information is generally accepted without sufficient questioning or structured analysis, limiting the ability to detect inconsistencies or propose improvements.

> **Expected:** Develop critical thinking skills by questioning assumptions, validating information, and exploring alternative approaches.

---

### 2.8 Meeting Leadership and Closure

During meetings, opportunities to reinforce leadership presence are sometimes missed, particularly in closing moments (e.g., no clear summary, next steps, or reopening for future discussions).

> **Expected:** Structure meeting conclusions by summarizing key points, confirming next steps, and creating clear openings for follow-up actions or future meetings.

---

## 3. Development Framework: Structured Thinking & Leadership

To support progression, the following structured approach is recommended:

### 3.1 Thinking and Decision-Making Checklist

Before providing an answer or making a decision:

1. What is the objective?
2. What do I know vs. what am I assuming?
3. What evidence supports this?
4. What are alternative options?
5. What could go wrong?

---

### 3.2 Questioning Framework (Minimum Expectation)

For each task or discussion, aim to ask at least three types of questions:

* **Clarity questions** → Ensure full understanding
* **Evidence questions** → Validate facts and data
* **Risk questions** → Anticipate potential issues

---

### 3.3 Communication Best Practices

* Ask **open-ended questions** (avoid yes/no questions)
* Use **reflective questions** (“What led to this outcome?”)
* Use **outcome-oriented questions** (“What should the result be?”)
* Provide **regular updates and escalations** when needed

---

### 3.4 Execution Discipline

* Systematically clarify tasks before starting
* Restate instructions to confirm alignment
* Introduce intermediate checkpoints
* Track progress actively
* Take initiative, even if decisions are not perfect

---

## 4. Conclusion

The current performance demonstrates strong engagement, reliability, and a positive inclination toward team interaction. These are valuable foundations for growth.

The next step is to evolve from a structured executor to a proactive leader by strengthening autonomy, critical thinking, and ownership. Developing these capabilities will significantly enhance overall impact and effectiveness in a leadership role.
